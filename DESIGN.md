# Cartridge — Brand Color Palette

---

## Logo Colors

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| **Cartridge Body** | `#C2C2CC` | Main cartridge body |
| **Cartridge Shadow** | `#4A4A60` | Outline, ridge details, indentations |

The slight blue tint in both pulls them into the same family as the navy foundation colors, keeping the logo cohesive on dark backgrounds.

---

## Wordmark

The wordmark is set in **Void** (`#0C1220`) on a pill-shaped background with a left-to-right gradient:

| Stop | Name | Hex |
|------|------|-----|
| Start | **Amber** | `#FFC753` |
| Mid | **Coral** | `#FF755F` |
| End | **Hot Pink** | `#FF3CAF` |

The pill sits horizontally across the middle of the cartridge. The warm gradient pops against the cool grey body, making the label the clear focal point of the logo.

## Foundation

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| **Void** | `#0C1220` | Primary dark background; also wordmark text color |
| **Deep Navy** | `#132044` | Secondary background, sidebars, cards |
| **Navy Mid** | `#1E3068` | Panels, elevated surfaces, borders, dividers |

---

## Accent System

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| **Amber** | `#FFC753` | Primary action — CTAs, links, active states, focus rings, ratings |
| **Coral** | `#FF755F` | Secondary accent — tags, badges, hover states |
| **Hot Pink** | `#FF3CAF` | Tertiary/special — featured content, Pro indicators, celebrations |

Amber does the heavy lifting. It's readable on dark backgrounds, signals action without screaming, and has natural games-industry warmth. Coral and Hot Pink are sparing — the more frequently they appear, the less special they feel.

---

## Status Colors

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| **Jade** | `#2DB870` | Success states, confirmations, "in collection" indicators |
| **Crimson** | `#E8384F` | Destructive actions, errors, warnings |

Crimson takes over destructive/error states from Coral, which is now a pure secondary accent. Jade is borrowed from Android's brand green — readable on dark navy backgrounds without going neon.

## Usage Guide

### Website (dark mode first)

- **Page background** — Void
- **Navigation** — Deep Navy; Amber active indicator
- **Cards / game entries** — Navy Mid surface, Primary Text, Amber for links and ratings
- **Primary CTA buttons** — Amber background, Void text
- **Secondary buttons** — Navy Mid background, Amber border + text
- **Destructive / warning states** — Crimson

### Logo Contexts

- **On dark backgrounds** — Cartridge Body + Shadow as-is; gradient pill wordmark
- **On light backgrounds (press kit, etc.)** — Body shifts to `#DDDDE6`, Shadow to `#5A5A70`
- **Monochrome** — Shadow color for the full cartridge, no label fill

### Typography Hierarchy

- **Page titles** — Primary Text; Amber for key emphasis words
- **Section headers** — Primary Text
- **Game titles** — Primary Text
- **Metadata** (year, genre, studio) — Secondary Text
- **Tags / genre pills** — Navy Mid background, Amber or Coral text

### Status & Data Indicators

- **Ratings / scores** — Amber
- **In collection / Played** — Jade
- **Wishlisted** — Coral
- **Destructive actions / errors** — Crimson
- **Featured / Editor's Pick** — Hot Pink accent

### Community-Facing Elements (feeds, labeler)

Lean on Amber and Coral more freely in social contexts — these are warmer, higher-energy surfaces. Hot Pink reserved for things like verified studio badges.

---

## Core Logic

**Navy is structure. Amber is signal. Coral and Hot Pink are personality.**

The cartridge logo's neutral grey body keeps it from competing with any background, while the gradient label is where it gets to have an opinion.
